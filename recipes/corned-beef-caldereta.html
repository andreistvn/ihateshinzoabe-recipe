Corned Beef Caldereta

Be it a party buffet or just a random dinner, Caldereta has been one of my favorite Filipino dishes. You just can't go wrong with a dish that has tender meat, a rich tomato sauce, and a hint of spice. BUT, a big BUT, it's so hard to make the beef tender and juicy! Imagine craving Caldereta after a long day, but you still have to wait for hours just to make the beef tender. It kills the mood, namsayin? Like the craving just goes away.

So, I thought, why not use corned beef? It's already tender and juicy, and it cooks faster than regular beef. And thus, the Corned Beef Caldereta was born. It's a quick and easy version of the classic Caldereta, but still packed with flavor. And the best part? You can make it in under 30 minutes! Really a godsend for us who doesn't have the time to wait for hours just to enjoy a good Caldereta. Enough with the talk, let's get to the recipe.

Ingredients

  • 1 can (340g) Delimondo Corned Beef (IMPORTANT)
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 medium potato, diced
  • 1 medium carrot, diced
  • 1/2 cup bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 cup beef broth or water
  • 1/4 cup liver spread (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Diced melt cheese (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat the cooking oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  2. Fry the potato and carrot first for an enhanced flavor and texture then set aside
  3. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, saut√© until fragrant.
  4. Stir in the Delimondo Corned Beef, breaking it up with a spoon but not too much so we can still have some chunks
  5. Add the diced bell pepper, beef broth (or water), and liver spread (if using).
  6. Sprinkle in the salt and pepper. Stir well to combine.
  7. Lower the heat, add the diced cheese and cover the pot to let it simmer.
  8. Once done, serve and enjoy it while still hot because you can only enjoy a good caldereta while it's hot

Note: Tomato sauce is not used in this recipe because the Delimondo Corned Beef already has a rich flavor and sauce. If you want to add more sauce, you can add more beef broth or water, but be careful not to make it too watery.
