Sisig

Sisig is a popular Filipino dish made from parts of pig's head and liver, seasoned with calamansi, onions, and chili peppers. It's known for its savory, tangy, and spicy flavors, often served on a sizzling plate. Sisig is typically enjoyed as a bar snack or main dish, and it's a favorite in many Filipino households and restaurants.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pig's head (ears, cheeks, and snout)
  • 200g pig's liver
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chili peppers, chopped
  • 3-4 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2-3 tablespoons vinegar
  • 1-2 tablespoons calamansi juice (or lemon juice)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • Optional: chopped green onions for garnish

Instructions

  1. Boil the pig's head in a large pot of water until tender, about 2-3 hours. Remove from heat and let it cool.
  2. Once cooled, chop the pig's head into small pieces, including the ears, cheeks, and snout.
  3. In a separate pan, heat the cooking oil over medium heat. Add the chopped pig's head pieces and cook until they are slightly crispy, about 10-15 minutes.
  4. Add the chopped pig's liver to the pan and cook until it is no longer pink, about 5-7 minutes.
  5. Stir in the chopped onion and green chili peppers, cooking until the onions are translucent.
  6. Pour in the soy sauce, vinegar, and calamansi juice. Mix well and let it simmer for another 5-10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve hot on a sizzling plate, garnished with chopped green onions if desired.
